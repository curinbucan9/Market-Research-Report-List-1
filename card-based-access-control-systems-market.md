<p><p>Card-based access control systems can be categorized into two main types: card readers with buttons and card readers without buttons. Card readers with buttons typically allow users to input additional information, such as a PIN or code, enhancing security by requiring two forms of authentication. In contrast, card readers without buttons streamline the access process by relying solely on card scanning, offering convenience and speed. Both types serve various security needs, suitable for different environments and user requirements.</p></p>
