<p><p>Corrosion inhibitors are substances that reduce the rate of corrosion in metals and are categorized mainly into two types: organic and inorganic. Organic inhibitors, often derived from carbon-based compounds, work by forming a protective film on metal surfaces, offering excellent resistance against rust and degradation. Inorganic inhibitors, typically mineral-based, enhance corrosion resistance through mechanisms like passivation or altering the electrochemical environment. Both types are essential in various industries, including oil and gas, construction, and automotive, to prolong equipment lifespan and reduce maintenance costs.</p></p>
