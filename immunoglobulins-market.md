<p><p>The immunoglobulins market comprises five main types: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, and IgD, each serving unique functions in the immune response. IgG dominates, providing long-term immunity and is widely used for therapeutic applications. IgA is crucial in mucosal immunity, while IgM is the first responder to infections. IgE plays a role in allergic reactions, and IgD is involved in B-cell activation. The market for these immunoglobulins is driven by rising autoimmune diseases, infectious diseases, and advancements in diagnostic technologies.</p></p>
