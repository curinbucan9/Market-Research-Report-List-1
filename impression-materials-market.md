<p><strong>The Impression Materials Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Alginates</li><li>Silicones</li><li>Polyethers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The impression materials market consists of three primary types: alginates, silicones, and polyethers. Alginates are hydrophilic, cost-effective, and ideal for preliminary impressions due to their easy handling and quick setting time. Silicones offer superior accuracy and flexibility, making them suitable for detailed impressions, especially in prosthodontics; they include addition-cured and condensation-cured varieties. Polyethers, known for their excellent dimensional stability and hydrophilicity, are used for precise impressions in challenging cases, often preferred in dental laboratories for their reliability.</p></p>
