<p><ul><li>Magnetostrictive Displacement</li><li>Conductive Plastic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Linear displacement sensors are critical in various applications for measuring linear movement. The magnetostrictive displacement sensor operates based on the principle of magnetostriction, utilizing a magnetic field to detect position, known for high accuracy and durability. In contrast, conductive plastic sensors use a resistive track to measure displacement through varying resistance, offering cost-effectiveness and simplicity in design. Both types serve industries such as automation, robotics, and automotive, catering to specific needs for precision and performance based on their operational principles.</p></p>
