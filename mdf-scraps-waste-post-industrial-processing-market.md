<p><p>The MDF scraps waste post-industrial processing market encompasses two main production types: dry and wet processes. The dry production process involves the mechanical treatment of MDF waste, resulting in the generation of fine particles or fibers without the use of water, making it energy-efficient. Conversely, the wet production process employs water as a medium to break down MDF scraps, facilitating the extraction of fibers and reducing dust emissions. Both methods contribute to sustainable practices by recycling MDF waste into useful products.</p></p>
