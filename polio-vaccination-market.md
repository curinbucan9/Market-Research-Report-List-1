<p><p>The polio vaccination market consists of two primary types: Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) and Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV). OPV is a live attenuated vaccine administered orally, offering ease of delivery and the potential for community immunity through vaccination. IPV, on the other hand, is an inactivated vaccine given via injection, providing robust individual protection without the risk of vaccine-derived poliovirus. Both vaccines play crucial roles in polio eradication efforts, catering to different health infrastructure and population needs.</p></p>
