<p><p>The vacuum gauge ball market includes various types of gauges used to measure vacuum pressure. The Shultz gauge operates on the principle of an elastic element, providing accurate low-pressure readings. The B-A gauge measures vacuum levels using a metal diaphragm for precise detection. Nude ion gauges use ionization to assess ultra-high vacuum levels, while Pirani gauges combine thermal conductivity principles for medium vacuum measurements. Thermocouple gauges utilize temperature differences to gauge vacuum pressure, making them suitable for a range of applications.</p></p>
