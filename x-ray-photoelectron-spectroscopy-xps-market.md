<p><p>X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS) is a surface analysis technique that measures the elemental composition and chemical states of materials by detecting emitted electrons after X-ray irradiation. The market for XPS is categorized into monochromatic and non-monochromatic types. Monochromatic XPS uses a single energy X-ray source, offering higher energy resolution and improved quantitative analysis. In contrast, non-monochromatic systems utilize a broader energy range, delivering faster results but often lower resolution. Both types cater to various applications in materials science, chemistry, and nanotechnology.</p></p>
